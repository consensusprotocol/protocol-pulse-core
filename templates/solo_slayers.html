How to Start Solo Mining (5-Minute Setup)

Point your Bitaxe at Solo CKPool and start generating lottery tickets. No registration required.

1

Get Your Hardware

Purchase a Bitaxe from bitaxe.org or authorized resellers. The Bitaxe Ultra (~500 GH/s) or Gamma (~1.2 TH/s) are popular choices. Cost: ~$150-300.

Pro tip: Multiple Bitaxes in a farm increase your tickets per 10-minute round.
2

Power Up & Connect

Connect your Bitaxe to power (USB-C, 5V 3A) and your WiFi network. Access the web interface at http://bitaxe.local or find its IP in your router.

Use a stable power supply—hashrate drops with unstable voltage.
3

Configure Solo Pool

In the Bitaxe settings, enter the Solo CKPool details:

Pool URL: solo.ckpool.org:3333
Username: YOUR_BITCOIN_ADDRESS
Password: x (or anything)
Use a fresh, cold-storage Bitcoin address. Your reward goes directly there—100% to you.
4

Start Mining

Click "Save" and your Bitaxe will connect to Solo CKPool. You'll see hashrate, accepted shares, and best difficulty hit on the dashboard.

Every ~10 minutes, a new block starts = a fresh lottery. Your shares are tickets.
5

Monitor Your Luck

Track your miner at solo.ckpool.org by entering your Bitcoin address. Watch your "Best Share" climb—when it exceeds network difficulty, you win!

Average wait at 500 GH/s: ~3,500 years. But someone wins every block—why not you?
